Focus Clock Narrative

Context: This sketch represents a “focus clock” designed to help users visualize their concentration time in an engaging and calming way. It supports students or remote workers who want to manage study sessions effectively.

Design decisions: I used a circular layout to symbolize continuous time. The color gradually shifts from bright green to orange to show progress. Minimal text and gentle animations maintain focus and calmness.

Future work: I plan to add features like automatic break reminders, haptic feedback, and customizable color themes.

Tree Growth Clock Narrative

Context: This sketch visualizes time through tree growth. Each hour helps the tree grow taller, turning time tracking into something organic and rewarding.

Who is this for? Designed for students or anyone practicing mindful productivity who prefers a nature-inspired metaphor.

Design decisions: Vertical layout mimics growth, with basic colors — green leaves, brown trunk, light background. Every hour restarts growth, symbolizing renewal.

Future work: Add seasonal changes and data tracking to build a personal “forest” of focus sessions.

Sketch Evolution

Fuel vs Burn: Are Your Meals Helping or Hurting?

Dataset: This visualization uses data from Life Style Data (Kaggle), which contains information on individuals’ diet types, daily calorie intake, activity levels, and health indicators such as BMI and weight changes.

Why this dataset: I chose this dataset because it directly connects to the theme of “Fuel vs Burn.” It provides measurable variables like calories consumed, calories burned, and diet type — perfect for comparing energy balance across different eating habits. The dataset also represents relatable, real-world behaviors that people can personally connect with, making the narrative both informative and self-reflective. Using this data allows the visualization to ground the metaphor (plates balancing intake and burn) in actual evidence rather than abstract examples, ensuring both clarity and credibility.

Context: This narrative visualization explores how different diet types balance calorie intake (“fuel”) and calorie burn (“burn”) using an intuitive metaphor — a set of plates. Each plate represents one diet type, comparing average calories consumed versus calories burned.

Why this story: People often track calories, but raw numbers feel abstract. This metaphor turns energy balance into a tangible visual — like a plate that tips toward health or excess. The question “Are your meals helping or hurting?” encourages reflection without blame.

User-driven vs Author-driven: This visualization is mainly author-driven because it presents a clear message comparing diets and calorie balance. However, it also includes user-driven aspects by allowing viewers to compare and interpret which diets seem balanced or excessive. This aligns with lecture content about balancing narrative control with user exploration.

Design decisions: I used a balancing plate metaphor for clarity and hierarchy. Red shows calories consumed, blue shows calories burned, and plate height indicates energy surplus or balance. The static format avoids distraction, emphasizing contrast and simplicity.

Hierarchy and clarity: Visual hierarchy guides attention:

  1. Title — frames the question and sets tone.
  2. Plates — encode key comparison (intake vs burn).
  3. Caption & legend — ensure correct interpretation.
Every element is purposeful, enhancing clarity and memorability.

Feedback and refinement: Early sketches used a tree metaphor, but peers said it lacked clear data and meal connection. I refined it into a “plate” visualization for stronger narrative alignment and immediate recognition. The simpler metaphor improved clarity and comparison.

User interviews:

  • Aurora: Understood the tree as calorie balance but wanted visible data labels and color distinction. → Added numeric calorie labels and consistent red/blue colors for clarity.
  • Ella: Found the tree metaphor confusing for a food story. → Inspired the shift to plates, aligning visuals with the “meal” concept and improving memorability.

Design principles applied: The design emphasizes clarity (minimal marks, clear labels), hierarchy (title → plate → caption), and memorability (concrete metaphor). It follows ethical visualization practices by presenting balanced comparisons without exaggeration.
